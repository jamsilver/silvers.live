Easter 2022

Happy Easter from James, Jen & Sophie!

Published
April 2022

A family of three have been sighted together in a Coventry dwelling, eyewitnesses say.

Well, somehow we've all made it through to the Easter hols! Phew!

It's been a strange old time for us. We managed to completely evade Covid until January this year when Sophie finally came down with the lurgy.

Fortunately it didn't hit her too hard. Just a few days of feeling a bit under the weather and then a lot of waiting for that line on the test to fade!

Poor Jen got hit by Covid and strep throat just a few weeks ago! A round of prescription antibiotics and a few days of rest were needed.

Last year, Sophie started Year 1 at Moseley Primary School.

Sophie has already reached a Stage 8 reading level. Well done!

Earlier this year we went for a flying visit to see James' parents and sister's family up in Crosby.

Shortly afterwards, Catherine and Graeme announced their engagement! They are to be wed in September later this year. We are very pleased for them!

In October Jen started a new job as a picker/packer at World of Books. It's working quite well for her although she is still thinking about other options.

Mike and Chris have also spent some time this year working hard on redecorating our bathroom. We are very grateful for this.

As for James, he recently joined Forte, a gospel-style choir, which he is enjoying very much. He's also started a book club with some friends.

He went for a random overnight jaunt up to go wild-swimming in Windermere in the Lake District with James Wright.

Hopefully we will return soon and have a go at crossing the lake!

Easter weekend

We spent this Easter with Jen's family up in Sheffield.

Please pray for us

  • HEALTH: In James' words this "feels stable but still not great". Jen has been taking Sertraline and Propanalol since April last year and it seems to be working well for her anxiety. James' weight is starting to balloon again, which is a worry.
  • US AS A FAMILY: We spent most of last year living separately but have been back living together since January this year. Things feel OK at the moment, but more stability would be nice!
  • BEDTIMES: Sophie seems to have a bit of fear about going to bed by herself at the moment, which is making bedtimes harder for all of us.
